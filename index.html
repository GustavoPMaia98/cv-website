About Me

I am a Ph.D. student and researcher at the Centro de Química Estrutural (CQE), working in collaboration with the Institut de Minéralogie, de Physique des Matériaux et de Cosmochimie (IMPMC) at the Muséum National d’Histoire Naturelle (MNHN) and with NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center (NASA-GSFC) on projects in prebiotic chemistry and astrobiology.

My research investigates the chemical origins and early evolution of life in the universe, using chemistry to uncover the processes and mechanisms that may have led to life’s emergence. A central theme of my work is mechanochemistry—the study of chemical reactions driven by mechanical forces. I explore how mechanical energy, such as that generated during parent-body formation, asteroid gardening, or meteorite impacts, can promote the synthesis and transformation of organic molecules under conditions relevant to the early Earth and other planetary bodies.

By reproducing and characterizing mechanically activated reactions and their products, I investigate alternative pathways for prebiotic chemistry and assess the potential role of mechanical activation in driving chemical complexity in extraterrestrial or extreme environments.
