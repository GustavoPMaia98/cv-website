Invited Assistant (University Teacher)
Sep 2024 – Oct 2024 · Instituto Superior Técnico, Portugal

As a Guest Teaching Assistant, I delivered and supervised practical laboratory sessions in General Chemistry for first-year undergraduate students in Aerospace Engineering. The practical lectures were designed to reinforce core chemical concepts through hands‑on experimentation, with a strong emphasis on laboratory safety, experimental rigour, and data interpretation.

The practical sessions covered fundamental topics, including solubility and miscibility, where students investigated phase behaviour, solvent–solute interactions, and the influence of temperature and polarity on compound dissolution. Additionally, I taught UV–visible spectroscopy, introducing students to basic spectroscopic principles, instrument operation, calibration procedures, and the quantitative analysis of chemical species using the Beer–Lambert law. Further practical instruction focused on chemical reaction kinetics, during which students studied reaction rates, the effects of concentration and temperature, and kinetic data analysis. I also supervised experiments related to corrosion processes, enabling students to explore electrochemical principles, corrosion mechanisms, and material degradation phenomena relevant to engineering applications.

Throughout these sessions, I guided students through experimental procedures, supported them in troubleshooting and error analysis, and encouraged critical thinking in the interpretation of results. I also contributed to the evaluation of laboratory reports and provided constructive feedback on experimental design, data presentation, and scientific writing. This teaching experience strengthened my skills in undergraduate instruction, scientific communication, and mentoring within an engineering‑focused academic environment.
